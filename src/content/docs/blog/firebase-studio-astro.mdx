Just leaving a little "status update" post as I clean up some content to no longer feature Gitpod (RIP) on this site.

## The more things change, the more they stay the same

There is not much new to say [about my use of online IDEs (notably Gitpod)](/blog/gitpod-astro/) since Gitpod shut down. In preparation of its impending demise, I [familiarized myself with IDX](/blog/idx-first-impressions/), Google's "Gitpod alternative" at the time.

Since then, Gitpod became Ona (and refocused its offerings away from public, open-source projects) and Project IDX became [Google Firebase Studio](https://firebase.studio/). I still code exclusively in the cloud, so I guess I'm the only one who hasn't rebranded!

I switch between a Chromebook and a Linux desktop frequently, even during the course of a single day. Both Gitpod and Firebase Studio were/are *very* reliable at saving my current state, to the keystroke, and allowing me to work on either machine (or both at the same time!) in the exact same environment. I can even add the 14" Samsung Android tablet into the mix! This was particularly important when my 7 year old Chromebook was prone to freezing up and crashing regularly.

I officially made the switch to Google's offering a year ago, and admittedly there's also not a lot more to say about Firebase Studio since its rebranding, 1 year in. It's still pretty dependable, with similarly dependable quirks, and it's still pretty slow. ðŸ˜‚

## Still my workhorse

So yeah, I think we're still lacking somewhat in the speed department, but I do have a lot of confidence that my edits are safe, even if not yet committed. It means that whatever is happening around me, I don't worry about losing data. Any device in my house can jump in and pick up in exactly the same spot, and I don't have to maintain updates or worry about anything OS-specific.

Right now, it solves more problems than it creates (kind of like me!) so it's still my daily driver.
